ROS2 Quadcopter Simulation

C++ | ROS2 | Gazebo

Development of a physics-based Quadcopter simulator with custom rigid-body physics engine, IMU sensor noise models, and camera visualization.

To truly master the mechanics of autonomous flight, I chose to move away from "black-box" physics engines and build my own. I developed a high-performance drone simulation (including RK4 integration and Physics), implemented entirely in Modern C++. This project serves as a robust testbed for flight control algorithms, allowing for deep customization of aerodynamic variables and sensor noise profiles.

Core Engineering Strengths:
  • Custom Physics Implementation: Developed a full rigid-body dynamics model from first principles, managing complex force and torque vectors without relying on third-party physics middleware.
  • High-Performance C++: Optimized the simulation loop in Modern C++ to ensure deterministic execution, critical for real-time control-loop prototyping.
  • Modular Architecture: Designed the system to be environment-agnostic; while currently integrated with Gazebo for visualization, the core physics remain decoupled and highly portable.
  • Sensor Fusion: I integrated the quadcopter’s onboard camera, bridging the data back into RViz for a real-time pilot's-eye view.
